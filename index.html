Assumptions & Limitations

▼

Assumptions

  • Each resident has the same independent annual probability of leaving, regardless of PGY year. In reality, attrition risk is highest during PGY-1 (~45% of departures) and lowest in PGY-3 (~20%).
  • The national average rate applies equally to all programs. Individual programs may run significantly above or below this average based on program culture, location, and other factors.
  • The attrition rate stays constant over the selected time window. Recent data suggest the rate may be trending upward (2.05% in AY 2023–25 vs. ~1.75% in AY 2020–23).
  • Departures are immediately replaced, keeping program size constant throughout the window. Some programs may carry vacancies.

Limitations

  • “Not completing program” in the ACGME data includes voluntary departures, transfers to other programs or specialties, dismissals, and leaves of absence that result in non-completion. It does not distinguish between these categories.
  • This is a population-level estimate. It cannot predict attrition for a specific resident or account for program-specific risk factors.
  • The formula P(≥1) = 1 − (1 − r)N×T treats attrition as a simple binomial process. More complex models (e.g., PGY-specific hazard rates, between-program heterogeneity) would yield slightly different estimates, though for typical FM program sizes the practical difference is modest.
  • Small programs (≤12 total residents) will experience high year-to-year variability around these estimates.
